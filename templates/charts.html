Observations

What does the Victoria Road Crash Dataset tell us?

Soooo Many Crashes

How many crashes are actually happening on Victorian roads? How does this amount change as the years progress?

Hypothesis

Crashes are expected to gradually decline as the years progress. This could be due to better car technology and more education and advertising about driving safer on the roads.

Results

There were a total of 74,530 crashes in Victoria between the years of 2010-2015. Interestingly, 2010 had the lowest number of car crashes with a sharp increase in crashes the years afterwards. Out of the regions, Western Victoria shows the most crashes for this time period.

Too Drunk to Stop?

Are the majority of hit and runs caused by drunk drivers? If there are more drunk drivers on the roads, will this mean there will be more hit and runs?

Hypothesis

It seems likely that as the number of drunk drivers increase on the roads, there would be a rise in hit and run incidents. This could be due to the fact that drunk drivers don't have the best sense of judgement, and don't want to be caught for two offences.

Results

The number of hit and runs tends to increase with the amount of drunk drivers on the roads. Not too surprising. The eastern suburbs represent this correlation the best. There is one outlier in a Western Region LGA, where there was only 68 drunk drivers but 346 hit and run incidents. It seems that sober drivers hit people too...

More Deaths During the Day

How does the light conditions on the roads affect the amount of fatalities on the roads? Are there more crashes in the dark?

Hypothesis

In darker situations and roads where there is little to no light, there should be an increased amount of crashes as it is harder for drivers to see other drivers or things on the road that could cause an accident.

Results

Car crashes tended to cause more fatalities in lighted environment rather than in the dark, contrary to what was expected. This is true even for crash crashes that happen in the night on lit roads. It is possible that there are more cars driving in lighter environments, therefore it would be more likely for crashes that cause fatalities to occur at these times.

Sorry About Your Car...

Which types of crashes are the most common in Victoria? How does this compare across different regions?

Hypothesis

Crashes with other cars on the road should tend to be the most common type of crash. This is probably due to there being so many cars on the road, that the easiest thing to hit would be a car.

Results

The most common type of crash, across all regions of Victoria is a collision with another vehicle. The Western region has the greatest percentage of crashes falling into this category, at 65.2%. Interestingly, northern Victoria has a much lower percentage of crashes that are collisions into another car, and instead crashes with a fixed object makes up a large portion of total crash types.

Tick Tock

Which times of day do most car crashes occur? What are the factors that determine which time of day are more prone to car crashes?

Hypothesis

During peak hours of travel, that amount of car crashes on the roads should spike. This is down the fact that there will be more cars on the roads, and therefore more car crashes.

Results

As expected, most car crashes occur during the daytime hours. There is an obvious increase in crashes at around 8:00am and again between 3:00pm - 5:00 pm, most likely as these are peak hours on the road, meaning there would be more cars on the roads at these times. All regions follow this tread pretty closely.
